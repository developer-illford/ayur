Pizhichil

Pizhichil is a luxurious and deeply therapeutic Ayurvedic treatment where warm medicated oil is continuously poured and massaged over the entire body.It combines Snehana (oleation) and Swedana (sudation) therapies.

Njavarakizhi

Njavarakizhi is a sudation therapy (Swedana) in which the whole body or specific parts are massaged using small boluses (pouches) filled with cooked Navara rice, a special type of medicinal rice cooked in herbal decoction and milk.

Abhyangam

A traditional Ayurvedic therapy featuring gentle, synchronized massage with warm herbal oils customized to your Dosha. Abhyangam nourishes the skin, boosts circulation, supports detoxification, relieves fatigue, and brings deep, calming relaxation to the body and mind.

Navarakizhi

A rejuvenating Ayurvedic therapy that uses warm pouches of Navara rice cooked in milk and herbal decoction to relieve joint pain, strengthen nerves, and restore vitality.

Swedana

The treatment in the image is called Swedana, specifically Bashpa Swedana, where the body is enclosed in a wooden steam chamber and exposed to herbal steam while the head remains outside. This Ayurvedic therapy helps the body release toxins, improves circulation.

WHAT IS AYURVEDA

AYURVEDA

Ayurveda is far more than a healing system. It is an all encompassing way of life that touches the physical, emotional, and spiritual self. Rather than simply treating ailments, it guides us toward balance by understanding our biological, psychological, and spiritual nature helping us live with greater vitality.

For over 25 years, we have shared this authentic tradition through meaningful wellness experiences in Kerala. What began as a personal journey between my wife and me has grown into trusted collaborations with three renowned centres across Kerala: Somatheeram Ayurveda Health Resort (Trivandrum), Marari Beach Resort (Alleppey), and Ayurvedam Sreenilayam (Calicut).

Ayurveda medicine and life philosophy

Ayurvedic medicine, with a history of over 5000 years, is the world’s oldest practised health system. Its name translates from Sanskrit as “knowledge of life.” An ancient definition from the Sushruta Samhita describes health as a state in which the body is in balance, digestion and metabolism work harmoniously, all systems function normally, and the soul and senses rest in lasting inner contentment. Interestingly, this aligns closely with the modern WHO definition of health as complete physical, mental, and social well-being.

This holistic vision is why Ayurveda has gained remarkable global recognition in recent decades. More people especially in industrialised nations are discovering that Ayurveda not only treats illness but also prevents it. Its therapies rejuvenate body and mind, enhance overall well-being, and restore strength weakened by modern lifestyles.
